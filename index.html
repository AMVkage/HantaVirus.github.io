The Hantaviruses are a family of viruses that spreads through rodents and has a 33% death rate. Americas are known as “New World” hantaviruses and may cause hantavirus pulmonary syndrome(HPS). Other hantaviruses, known as “Old World” hantavirus, are found mostly in Europe and Asia and may cause hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome(HFRS). The hantaviruses can spread through the urine, feces, and saliva of rodents. The most important hantavirus in the US that can cause HPS is the Sin Nombre virus, spread by the deer mouse.
